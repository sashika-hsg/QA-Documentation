
Each folder represents a **distinct QA responsibility area** and includes a local `README.md` explaining intent and usage.

## Key Folders Explained

### 01_Test_Strategy
Enterprise-level QA strategy artefacts defining:
- Test approach and scope
- Risk-based testing principles
- Entry and exit criteria
- Alignment with delivery and release goals

---

### 02_Compliance_and_Traceability
Artefacts focused on:
- Requirement and compliance traceability
- Evidence mapping
- Audit and regulatory readiness
- Risk visibility in regulated environments

---

### 03_Execution_and_Reporting
Operational QA artefacts covering:
- Test execution summaries
- Defect triage and root cause analysis
- Risk reporting and visibility

---

### 04_Quality_Metrics
Documentation defining:
- Meaningful QA metrics
- Risk and quality indicators
- Reporting aligned to decision-making, not vanity metrics

---

### 05_Agile_and_Release
QA governance artefacts including:
- Requirement Capture & Validation Workflow
- Sprint QA Checklist
- Release Readiness Checklist
- Test governance and decision frameworks

This folder represents **QA ownership across the delivery lifecycle**.

---

### 06_API_and_Integration
Templates and guidance for:
- API testing
- Integration validation
- Data integrity checks
- System-to-system behaviour validation

---

### 07_Automation
Automation-focused artefacts covering:
- Automation strategy
- Framework blueprints
- BDD-style templates
- Guidance on what to automate and when

---

### 08_AI_and_Advanced_Testing
Advanced QA artefacts addressing:
- AI / ML validation
- Human-in-the-loop testing
- Explainability and confidence validation
- Risk management for AI-assisted systems

---

### 09_Non_Functional
Non-functional QA templates including:
- Accessibility testing
- Security (OWASP) considerations
- Performance and resilience guidance

These are included to demonstrate **breadth and future readiness**, even where not actively implemented.

---

### pdf/
This folder contains **PDF versions of selected documents** for:
- Recruiter review
- Interview sharing
- LinkedIn Featured content

> Markdown files remain the **authoritative source of truth**.  
> PDFs are provided as **presentation artefacts**.
